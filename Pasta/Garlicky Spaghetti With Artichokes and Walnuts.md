# Garlicky Spaghetti With Artichokes and Walnuts

Jarred artichoke hearts, slowly toasted garlic, and crunchy walnuts transform spaghetti into an elegant pantry pasta — quick enough for weeknights but impressive enough for company.

### 🛒 Ingredients
- 6 medium cloves garlic, minced (about 2 tablespoons)
- ¼ cup (60 ml) extra-virgin olive oil
- 1½ teaspoons fennel seed, coarsely ground in a mortar and pestle or spice grinder
- ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes
- 1 (14-ounce) can or jar artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
- 1 pound (453 g) spaghetti
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 ounce (28 g) Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated (½ cup), plus extra for serving
- ½ cup walnuts, toasted and chopped
- 1 tablespoon (15 ml) fresh lemon juice
- 2 tablespoons chopped flat-leaf parsley

### 🥣 Steps
1. In a 10-inch skillet, combine garlic and oil and cook over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the garlic is pale golden brown, 6 to 9 minutes. Off heat, stir in the fennel seeds and pepper flakes until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Stir in the artichokes; set aside.
2. In a pot or large sauté pan of salted boiling water, cook the spaghetti, stirring frequently, until al dente. Reserve 1 cup cooking water, then drain the pasta and return it to the pot. Scrape in the reserved artichoke-garlic oil, then add the cheese, walnuts, lemon juice, and ½ cup reserved cooking water. Stir until the pasta is well coated and no water remains in the bottom of the pot, adjusting with more cooking water as needed. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Transfer to serving dishes and sprinkle with parsley and extra cheese.

> [!tip] Tip
> Toast the garlic gently over low heat to pale gold, not brown — burnt garlic turns bitter. The starchy reserved pasta water is what emulsifies the oil and cheese into a glossy sauce, so add it gradually.
