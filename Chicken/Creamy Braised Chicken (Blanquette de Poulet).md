# Creamy Braised Chicken (Blanquette de Poulet)

A French-inspired braise of tender bone-in chicken thighs, bacon, and vegetables in a silky white wine sauce finished with cream. Comforting and elegant at once, yet weeknight-friendly.

### 🛒 Ingredients
- 2 slices thick-cut bacon (2 ounces; 56 g), cut into ¼-inch pieces
- 2 pounds (908 g) bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs, patted dry
- Kosher salt and black pepper to taste
- 8 ounces (227 g) cremini mushrooms, stemmed and sliced into ¼-inch pieces
- ½ medium yellow onion (about 4 ounces; 113 g), finely diced
- 1 large carrot (about 4 ounces; 113 g), cut into a ½-inch dice
- 1 large celery rib (about 2 ounces; 56 g), finely diced
- 1 cup dry white wine (8 ounces; 240 ml), such as riesling or sauvignon blanc
- 1 cup (240 ml) low-sodium chicken broth, homemade or store-bought, or water
- 4 teaspoons (9 g) cornstarch
- ½ cup (120 ml) heavy cream
- Crusty bread, egg noodles, or cooked farro, for serving

### 🥣 Steps
1. In a 5-quart Dutch oven set over medium-low heat, cook the bacon, stirring occasionally, until the fat has rendered and the bacon is crispy, about 8 minutes. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the bacon to a small bowl. Increase heat to medium-high.
2. Lightly season the chicken all over with salt and pepper, then place it skin-side-down in the Dutch oven. Cook until well browned, 5 to 7 minutes per side. Using tongs, remove the chicken to a plate. Pour off all but 2 tablespoons of fat from the Dutch oven; discard the excess.
3. Add the mushrooms, onion, carrots, and celery and cook over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and the onion and celery are translucent, about 7 minutes. Scrape up the fond from the bottom and sides of the pot. Return the bacon and chicken to the pan, nestling the chicken skin-side-up among the vegetables. Add the wine and broth (or water) and season generously. (Liquid should reach about ⅔ of the way up the sides of the chicken; add more water as needed.)
4. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low. Cover and gently simmer until the vegetables are soft and the meat is tender, 45 to 55 minutes. Using tongs, remove the chicken to a plate; set aside.
5. Bring the braising liquid to a boil over medium-high and cook, stirring frequently, until slightly reduced, about 5 minutes.
6. In a small bowl, whisk the cornstarch with 3 tablespoons cold water. Pour the slurry into the braising liquid, stir well, and cook until the sauce is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, about 3 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the cream until well combined. To serve, place individual thighs onto plates and ladle the vegetables and sauce over the top. Serve with crusty bread, egg noodles, or cooked farro.

> [!tip] Tip
> Get a deep, even browning on the chicken skin in step 2 — that fond is the backbone of the sauce's flavor. Stir the cream in off the heat so it stays silky and doesn't break.
