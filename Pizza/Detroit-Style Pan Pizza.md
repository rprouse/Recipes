# Detroit-Style Pan Pizza

Detroit-style pizza is its own thing: a thick, airy pan pizza topped with brick cheese, pepperoni, and red sauce, with a lacy, crispy cheese crust caramelized all the way around the edges. Here's how to make it at home.

### 🛒 Ingredients
- 300g bread flour (10.5 ounces; about 2 generous cups)
- 5g instant yeast (0.15 ounce; about 1 teaspoon), such as SAF Instant Yeast
- 9g salt (0.3 ounce; about 1½ teaspoons table salt or 1 tablespoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt)
- 220g water (7.75 ounces; about 1 cup minus 1½ teaspoons)
- Extra-virgin olive oil, as needed
- 2 tablespoons (30ml) extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 medium cloves garlic, minced
- 2 teaspoons (about 5g) dried oregano
- Dash red pepper flakes
- 1 (28-ounce; 800g) can high-quality crushed tomatoes
- 1 teaspoon (about 6g) granulated garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon (about 6g) granulated onion powder
- 1 tablespoon (about 15g) sugar
- Kosher salt, to taste
- 12 ounces (340g) brick cheese, cut into ½-inch cubes (see tip)
- 12 ounces (340g) high-quality natural-casing pepperoni, such as Boar's Head or Vermont Smoke & Cure, cut into ⅛-inch slices (optional)

### 🥣 Steps
1. **Dough (stand mixer):** Combine flour, yeast, and salt in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook. Stir to combine, then add water. Mix on low until the dough comes together into a rough ball, shut off the mixer, and rest 10 minutes. Mix at medium-low until the dough forms a smooth, silky ball, about 10 minutes longer (it should stick to the bottom of the bowl rather than ride the edges). Form into a tight ball in the bowl, cover tightly with plastic, and let rise in a warm place until roughly doubled, about 2 hours. *(Food processor: pulse dry ingredients, add water, process ~30 seconds until a ball rides the bowl, then 30 seconds more. By hand: whisk dry ingredients, stir in water to a rough ball, rest 10 minutes, then knead ~10 minutes. Both rise the same 2 hours.)*
2. Pour a couple tablespoons of olive oil into the bottom of a Detroit-style anodized aluminum pan (or two 8-by-8-inch cake pans; split the dough in half if using cake pans). Transfer dough to the pan and turn to coat in oil. Press and spread it toward the edges — you won't reach them yet, and that's okay. Cover tightly and rest 30 minutes to relax the dough, then stretch again; it should reach the edges this time. To hold the corners, stretch up beyond them so the dough pulls back into place. Cover and set aside while you make the sauce.
3. **Sauce:** Adjust an oven rack to the lowest position and preheat to 550°F (290°C), or as close as your oven gets. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium until shimmering. Add minced garlic, oregano, and pepper flakes; cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add tomatoes, garlic powder, onion powder, and sugar. Bring to a simmer and cook until reduced to about 3 cups, about 30 minutes. Season to taste with salt.
4. **Form the pizza:** Press down on the dough with your fingertips to remove large air bubbles. Lay half the pepperoni (if using) evenly over the dough. Top with the cheese, spreading it all the way to the very edges of the pan, then add the remaining pepperoni. Spoon sauce over the surface in 3 even rows. (You'll need only about half the sauce — save the rest for another pizza.)
5. **Bake:** Transfer to the oven and bake until the edges are black and bubbly and the exposed cheese on top is starting to lightly brown, 12 to 15 minutes. (If your oven doesn't cook well from the bottom, place the pizza directly on the oven floor.) Transfer to a trivet or folded towel.
6. Run a thin metal spatula all the way around the edges of the pan to loosen the pizza. Carefully lift it out, slide it onto a cutting board, cut, and serve.

> [!tip] Tip
> Brick cheese is traditional and melts with the right stretch and browning; if you can't find it, Wisconsin brick or a mix of Monterey Jack and low-moisture mozzarella stands in well. Push the cheese all the way to the pan edges — that's what creates the signature lacy, caramelized crust.
