# 10 Pizza Rules That Separate Amateurs from Pros
This recipe focuses on creating a perfect Neapolitan pizza by emphasizing important rules for dough, sauce, and cooking techniques.

## Neapolitan Pizza

### Ingredients
- 900 g all-purpose organic bread flour
- 100 g whole wheat flour
- 700 g water
- 15 g salt
- 1 tsp instant yeast
- Canned Roma or San Marzano tomatoes (amount as needed for sauce)
- Olive oil (for drizzling)
- Fresh basil (to taste)
- Parmesan cheese (to taste)
- Fresh mozzarella (to taste)

### Steps
1. Combine 900 g of all-purpose organic bread flour and 100 g of whole wheat flour in a mixing bowl.
2. Add 700 g of water to the flour mixture.
3. Incorporate 15 g of salt and 1 tsp of instant yeast into the dough mixture.
4. Mix thoroughly for about 10 minutes using a mixer or by hand until fully combined.
5. Once mixed, transfer the dough to a work surface and perform a few stretch and folds.
6. Let the dough rest for approximately 20 minutes, then fold it again to strengthen it.
7. Place the dough in a container to track fermentation; allow it to rise at room temperature for about 3 to 4 hours, or refrigerate for 12 to 24 hours.
8. After the dough has doubled in volume, divide it into 250 g balls and place them in a proofing bin, either oiled or dusted with flour.
9. Optionally, refrigerate the dough balls for another 12 to 24 hours or let them sit at room temperature for 1 to 2 hours to fully proof.
10. For the sauce, take the canned tomatoes, crush them by hand, and add salt to taste. Optionally, cook them in olive oil with garlic for added flavor, reducing to desired consistency.
11. Preheat your pizza oven to a maximum temperature (ideally around 900°F for Neapolitan).
12. Dust a pizza peel with semolina or flour and place the proofed dough ball on it.
13. Shape the dough while ensuring to lock in air in the crust.
14. Add the prepared sauce evenly over the dough, followed by a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese and parts of fresh mozzarella.
15. Optionally, add fresh basil leaves, drizzling with olive oil to prevent burning.
16. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for about 3 minutes, keeping an eye on browning and turning as necessary.
17. Once the crust is bubbly and golden, remove the pizza, let it cool slightly, and serve. Enjoy your homemade Neapolitan pizza!
