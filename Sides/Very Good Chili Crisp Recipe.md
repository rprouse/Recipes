# Very Good Chili Crisp Recipe
This homemade chili crisp is packed with flavor, featuring crispy garlic and shallots infused in a rich, spicy oil. It's perfect as a condiment for a variety of dishes.

# Very Good Chili Crisp

## Ingredients
- 700 g peanut oil
- 75 g garlic, evenly sliced
- 200 g shallots, evenly sliced
- 75-100 g ginger, sliced into large coins
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 5-6 star anise pods
- 60 g Korean gochugaru chili powder
- 30 g regular chili flakes
- 20 g sesame seeds
- Cracked Sichuan peppercorns (to taste)
- 75 g chopped peanuts
- 35 g salt
- 5 g MSG
- 3 g fish sauce
- 25 g soy sauce
- 40 g honey

## Steps
1. Heat the peanut oil in a 3 to 4-quart pot to around 300°F (150°C).
2. Fry the sliced garlic in the hot oil for about 2 and a half minutes until light golden and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
3. Increase the oil temperature back to 300°F and fry the sliced shallots for 5 to 6 minutes until light golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels with the garlic.
4. Add the ginger coins to the oil and fry for 3 to 5 minutes, allowing them to infuse flavor into the oil.
5. Add the cinnamon sticks and star anise to the oil. Fry for an additional 3 to 5 minutes to further infuse the oil.
6. In a medium bowl, combine the gochugaru chili powder with some of the infused oil from the pot, pouring it through a strainer to catch the ginger and spices.
7. Allow this mixture to steep for 5 minutes to enhance the chili flavor.
8. Strain out the solids from the oil, then return to low heat and bring the oil up to 325°F (160°C).
9. In the same bowl, mix in the remaining gochugaru, chili flakes, sesame seeds, and cracked Sichuan peppercorns.
10. Carefully pour the hot oil over the chili mixture and stir well to combine.
11. Crumble the fried garlic and shallots into the bowl. Optional: chop them further for a finer texture.
12. Add the chopped peanuts, salt, MSG, fish sauce, soy sauce, and honey. Stir until everything is well combined.
13. Transfer the chili crisp into containers, ensuring to leave some space for expansion if freezing.
14. Refrigerate for at least one day to allow the flavors to meld. The chili crisp will keep for about 4 to 5 weeks.

This chili crisp is great on rice, dumplings, avocado toast, and more!
