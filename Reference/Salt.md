# Salt — Types & When to Use

> Part of [[Cooking Reference]]

Salt is the single most important seasoning, but not all salts behave the same. The big differences are **crystal size** (which changes how much fits in a spoon and how fast it dissolves) and **additives**. By weight all salt is about equally salty — but by volume a tablespoon of fine salt is far saltier than a tablespoon of flaky salt, which is why recipes should ideally specify *which* salt.

| Salt Type | Common Names / Brands | Best Uses |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Table salt** (fine, dense) | Iodized table salt | Baking (precise measuring), general cooking in a pinch — use sparingly, it's the "saltiest" by volume |
| **Kosher salt** (coarse flakes) | Diamond Crystal, Morton | The everyday cooking salt — seasoning meat, salting pasta water, general cooking. Easy to pinch and sprinkle evenly |
| **Fine sea salt** | Assorted | All-purpose cooking and baking; dissolves quickly into sauces, doughs, and brines |
| **Flaky finishing salt** | Maldon, fleur de sel | Sprinkled *at the end* for crunch and pop — on steaks, salads, chocolate, caramel, roasted veg |
| **Coarse / rock salt** | Sel gris, rock salt | Brining, salt crusts, salt grinders, packing (not for direct seasoning) |
| **Specialty salts** | Himalayan pink, smoked, Hawaiian black/red | Finishing and presentation; smoked salt adds a BBQ note |

### 🧂 Key tips
- **Diamond Crystal ≠ Morton.** Diamond Crystal kosher is much less dense — about **half** as salty by volume as Morton. If a recipe says "kosher salt" and doesn't specify, start light and taste.
- **Salt early** for seasoning *into* food (meat, water, sauces) and **finish with flaky salt** for texture and a bright hit of flavour.
- **Salt pasta water generously** — it should taste like the sea. Most of it drains away; it seasons the pasta itself.
- **Iodized vs not:** iodine is a valuable nutrient but can add a faint metallic taste in large amounts; many cooks prefer non-iodized for cooking and pickling.

> [!tip] Rule of thumb
> Cook with **kosher salt**, bake with **fine salt**, and finish with **flaky salt**. Season in layers and taste as you go — you can always add more, but you can't take it out.
