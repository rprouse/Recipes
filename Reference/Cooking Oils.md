# Cooking Oils & Smoke Points

> Part of [[Cooking Reference]]

An oil's **smoke point** is the temperature at which it starts to break down, smoke, and taste acrid — so it decides which oil suits which job. **High smoke point** oils are for high-heat cooking (searing, deep-frying, stir-frying); **low smoke point** oils are for finishing, dressings, and low heat, where their flavour is the point.

| Oil | Approx. Smoke Point | Best Uses |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Avocado oil** | ~270 °C / 520 °F | Highest-heat searing, deep-frying, roasting; neutral flavour |
| **Refined / light olive oil** | ~240 °C / 465 °F | High-heat frying and roasting; neutral-ish |
| **Canola / vegetable oil** | ~205 °C / 400 °F | Everyday all-purpose frying, sautéing, baking; neutral and cheap |
| **Peanut oil** | ~230 °C / 450 °F | Deep-frying (esp. fries, chicken), stir-frying |
| **Grapeseed oil** | ~215 °C / 420 °F | Sautéing, frying, vinaigrettes; clean neutral flavour |
| **Refined coconut oil** | ~200 °C / 400 °F | Frying, baking, sautéing; faint coconut note |
| **Extra-virgin olive oil (EVOO)** | ~190 °C / 375 °F | Everyday sautéing, roasting, dressings, finishing — flavour-forward |
| **Butter** | ~150 °C / 300 °F | Low-medium sautéing, sauces, baking; browns and burns easily |
| **Ghee / clarified butter** | ~230 °C / 450 °F | Higher-heat cooking with butter flavour (milk solids removed) |
| **Toasted sesame oil** | ~175 °C / 350 °F | Finishing & flavouring only — add off the heat, not for frying |

### 🫒 Key tips
- **Match oil to heat, not price.** Save flavourful EVOO and toasted sesame oil for finishing; use neutral high-smoke-point oils for searing and frying.
- **EVOO for high heat is fine** for home cooking despite the myth — it's flavour, not safety, that fades. Just don't push it to smoking.
- **Refined vs unrefined:** refining raises the smoke point but strips flavour. "Light" olive oil is refined, not lower in calories.
- **If oil smokes, it's too hot** — it's begun to degrade and will taste bitter. Pull the pan off, let it cool, or start over.
- **Butter + oil together** lets you get butter flavour at a slightly higher temperature before the milk solids burn.

### 🗄️ Storage
- Keep oils in a **cool, dark cupboard** — light and heat turn them rancid. Buy in sizes you'll use within a few months.
- **Nut and seed oils** (walnut, toasted sesame, flax) go rancid fastest — store in the fridge and use quickly.

> [!tip] Rule of thumb
> **High heat → avocado, peanut, canola.** **Everyday → olive oil.** **Flavour/finish → EVOO, sesame, butter.** If it's smoking, it's too hot for that oil.
