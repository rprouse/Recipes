# Onions & Alliums

> Part of [[Cooking Reference]]

The allium family — onions, garlic, leeks, shallots, and their cousins — forms the aromatic backbone of most savoury cooking. They range from sharp and pungent raw to sweet and mellow when cooked slowly. Choosing the right one is mostly about **how strong** you want the flavour and **whether it's cooked or raw**.

| Allium | Also Known As | Best Uses |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Yellow onion** | Brown onion, cooking onion | The default all-purpose onion — sautéing, soups, stews, caramelizing, stocks |
| **White onion** | — | Sharper and cleaner than yellow; Mexican cooking, salsas, raw garnishes |
| **Red onion** | Purple onion | Raw uses — salads, sandwiches, pickling; also good grilled or roasted |
| **Sweet onion** | Vidalia, Walla Walla, Maui | Mild and sweet; onion rings, raw in salads, roasting |
| **Shallot** | — | Vinaigrettes, pan sauces, finely minced where you want mild onion + garlic notes |
| **Green onion** | Scallion, spring onion | Raw garnish, stir-fries, quick sautés; use both white and green parts |
| **Leek** | — | Soups (potato-leek), braises, gentle sautéed base; sweeter and milder than onion |
| **Garlic** | — | Universal aromatic — raw for punch, roasted for sweet mellow depth |
| **Chives** | — | Fresh herb-like finish on eggs, potatoes, soups; mild onion flavour |

### 🧅 Key tips
- **Sweetness comes from cooking.** Raw onion is sharp; low-and-slow cooking (caramelizing) converts its harshness into deep sweetness over 30–45 min.
- **Tame raw onion** by soaking sliced onion in cold water for 10 min — it removes the harsh bite for salads and sandwiches.
- **Garlic burns fast.** Add it after onions, or near the end of sautéing; browned garlic turns bitter in seconds.
- **Clean leeks well** — grit hides between the layers. Slice, then rinse in a bowl of water so the sand sinks.
- **Green tops of scallions** are milder and best raw; the white bottoms take more cooking.

### 🗄️ Storage
- Store **whole onions, shallots, and garlic** in a cool, dark, dry, well-ventilated spot — **not** the fridge (they go soft) and **not** next to potatoes (each spoils the other).
- **Scallions, leeks, and chives** are fresh produce — keep them in the fridge; stand scallions upright in a little water to last longer.
- **Cut onions** go in a sealed container in the fridge; use within a few days.

> [!tip] Rule of thumb
> Reach for a **yellow onion** unless the recipe says otherwise. Want it raw and mellow? **Red or sweet.** Want subtle and elegant? **Shallot or leek.**
