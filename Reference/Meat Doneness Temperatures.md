# Meat Doneness Temperatures

> Part of [[Cooking Reference]]

A reliable **instant-read thermometer** is the only sure way to judge doneness — colour and touch lie. Temperatures below are the internal temp at the thickest part. Note the difference between **food-safety minimums** (what public-health bodies recommend) and the **cook's target** many people actually pull at for the best texture. Always account for **carryover cooking**: large cuts rise **5–10 °F / 3–5 °C** while resting, so pull them early.

### 🥩 Beef, Lamb & Steaks (whole cuts)
| Doneness | Pull Temp | Final (after rest) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rare | 49 °C / 120 °F | 52 °C / 125 °F |
| Medium-rare *(recommended)* | 52 °C / 125 °F | 57 °C / 135 °F |
| Medium | 57 °C / 135 °F | 63 °C / 145 °F |
| Medium-well | 63 °C / 145 °F | 68 °C / 155 °F |
| Well done | 68 °C / 155 °F | 71 °C+ / 160 °F+ |

_USDA safe minimum for whole beef/lamb/pork: **63 °C / 145 °F** with a 3-minute rest._

### 🐷 Pork
| Cut | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Chops, roasts, tenderloin | **63 °C / 145 °F** + 3-min rest (slightly pink and juicy) |
| Ground pork | 71 °C / 160 °F |
| Ribs / pulled pork (low & slow) | 88–96 °C / 190–205 °F (collagen breaks down) |

### 🐔 Poultry (chicken & turkey)
| Cut | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Breast (white meat) | **74 °C / 165 °F** — or pull at 68 °C/155 °F and hold; carryover finishes it, keeping it juicy |
| Thighs / legs (dark meat) | 74–85 °C / 165–185 °F — higher is more tender |
| Ground chicken/turkey | 74 °C / 165 °F |
| Whole bird | 74 °C / 165 °F at the thickest part of the thigh |

### 🐟 Fish & Seafood
| Item | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Most fish | 52–57 °C / 125–135 °F (flakes, still moist) |
| Salmon | 49–52 °C / 120–125 °F for medium; 63 °C/145 °F for well |
| Tuna (seared) | 43–46 °C / 110–115 °F (rare centre) |
| Shrimp / lobster / scallops | Opaque and firm; ~50–54 °C / 120–130 °F |

### 🌡️ Key tips
- **Pull early, rest.** Carryover cooking keeps raising the temp after you take the meat off the heat — resting also lets juices redistribute.
- **Measure the thickest part**, away from bone, fat, and the pan surface.
- **Ground meats need higher temps** than whole cuts — grinding spreads any surface bacteria throughout.
- **Rest times:** thin steaks ~5 min, roasts and whole birds 15–20+ min under loose foil.
- **The 165 °F chicken rule is about time-at-temp.** Chicken held at 155 °F for ~1 minute is just as safe and noticeably juicier — useful if you have a good thermometer.

> [!tip] Rule of thumb
> **Beef/lamb steaks → 52 °C/125 °F for medium-rare. Pork → 63 °C/145 °F. Chicken → 74 °C/165 °F.** Pull a few degrees early and let it rest.
