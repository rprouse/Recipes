# The Only Naan Recipe You Need |  No Oven, Restaurant-Like Results

Discover how to make soft, flavorful naan breads right at home with minimal ingredients and no oven needed. This simple recipe delivers restaurant-quality garlic and coriander naan with perfect toasty marks using a stovetop method.

# Naan Bread

## Ingredients

- 500 g self-raising flour  
- 1 teaspoon kalonji seeds (onion seeds)  
- 1 level teaspoon baking powder  
- 1 level teaspoon salt  
- 200 ml tepid warm full-fat milk  
- 1 egg  
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar (white sugar can be used)  
- 2 tablespoons olive oil  
- 2 tablespoons Greek yogurt (5% fat recommended)  
- Oil for greasing hands  
- Butter ghee (for brushing after cooking)  

## Steps

1. Mix kalonji seeds, baking powder, and salt into the self-raising flour.  
2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warm milk, egg, sugar, olive oil, and Greek yogurt until fully combined.  
3. Make a well in the dry ingredients and pour in the wet mixture gradually, starting with about a third. Mix gently to start forming dough, then add remaining liquid in thirds.  
4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 5 to 7 minutes until smooth. The dough should be slightly wet and sticky, not dry.  
5. Lightly brush dough with oil, cover with a tea towel, and leave in a warm place to rise for 2 hours (1 hour if pressed for time).  
6. After rising, roll the dough into a log shape and divide into 5 to 7 equal portions depending on preferred naan size.  
7. Lightly flour the surface and your rolling pin. Roll each portion into a teardrop or round shape about ⅛ inch thick.  
8. Heat a cast iron tawa or thick pan over medium heat for 2 minutes, then turn heat to medium-high to get it very hot.  
9. Dab a little water on one side of the naan and place this wet side down on the hot tawa. Bubbles will start to form.  
10. Once bubbles cover the surface, flip the naan and toast the other side 8 inches above the flame or on the pan until golden and charred in spots.  
11. Remove with a metal spatula, brush with butter ghee, and serve warm.  

# Garlic and Coriander Naan

## Ingredients

- 2 cloves garlic  
- 2 heaped tablespoons butter ghee  
- Fresh coriander leaves  

## Steps

1. Slice garlic cloves thinly down the middle.  
2. Melt butter ghee in a small pan over medium heat. Add garlic slices and fry until they turn golden brown and nutty—avoid raw garlic chunks.  
3. Remove garlic from ghee, finely chop or mash it, then stir chopped garlic back into the garlic-infused ghee.  
4. After cooking and brushing plain naan with butter ghee, spoon the garlic ghee over the naan while still hot.  
5. Sprinkle a few fresh coriander leaves sparingly on top.  
6. Serve immediately for the best flavor and texture.
