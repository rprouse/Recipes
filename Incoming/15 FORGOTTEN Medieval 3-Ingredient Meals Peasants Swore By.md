# 15 FORGOTTEN Medieval 3-Ingredient Meals Peasants Swore By
Discover the simplicity and nutrition of medieval peasant meals that only require three ingredients each.

# Peas Pottage
A thick and creamy dish made with soaked dried peas, this meal was a staple for English peasants.

Ingredients
- Dried field peas
- Water
- Salt

Steps
1. Soak dried field peas overnight.
2. Simmer the soaked peas in a pot of water over low heat for about 2 hours until they break down into a thick mass.
3. Add a pinch of salt to finish.

# Bread and Dripping
A quick and calorie-dense meal featuring bread spread with rendered animal fat.

Ingredients
- Bread
- Rendered animal fat
- Salt

Steps
1. Spread rendered fat on a thick slice of bread.
2. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt.
3. Enjoy with your hands.

# Frumenty
This wheat porridge sustained medieval populations with its hearty ingredients.

Ingredients
- Whole wheat grains
- Water
- Milk

Steps
1. Boil cracked or whole wheat grains in water until they burst and soften.
2. Stir in milk to finish the dish and serve.

# Cabbage Pottage
A simple vegetable stew that's easy to prepare and packed with vitamins.

Ingredients
- Cabbage
- Onions
- Water

Steps
1. Chop cabbage and slice onions.
2. Combine both in a pot of water and simmer until soft.

# Bean Pottage
A nutritious meal that made meat optional for medieval families.

Ingredients
- Dried fava beans
- Water
- Onions

Steps
1. Simmer dried fava beans in a pot of water until cooked through.
2. Stir in chopped onions and simmer until all ingredients are tender.

# Sops
A clever way to utilize stale bread while creating a filling and nutritious meal.

Ingredients
- Stale bread
- Hot broth
- (Optional: Salt)

Steps
1. Crumble stale bread into a bowl.
2. Pour hot broth over the bread until softened.
3. Sprinkle with salt if desired and enjoy.

# Oat Porridge
A hearty breakfast that fuels a long day of labor.

Ingredients
- Oats
- Water
- Salt

Steps
1. Boil water in a pot.
2. Stir in oats and cook until thickened.
3. Add salt to taste.

# Turnip Pottage
A nutritious and comforting stew featuring root vegetables.

Ingredients
- Turnips
- Water
- Onions

Steps
1. Peel and dice turnips and slice onions.
2. Combine in a pot of water and simmer until everything is tender.

# Maslin Bread and Cheese
A filling midday meal combining homemade bread and cheese.

Ingredients
- Maslin bread (wheat and rye mix)
- Hard cheese
- Water

Steps
1. Slice a piece of maslin bread.
2. Serve with a slab of hard cheese.

# Leek and Barley Stew
A medicinal meal recommended for its digestive and respiratory benefits.

Ingredients
- Leeks
- Barley
- Water

Steps
1. Chop leeks and combine with barley in a pot of water.
2. Simmer until the barley is tender and the stew thickens.

# Herb Pottage
A nourishing dish made from foraged greens, perfect for resourceful households.

Ingredients
- Foraged greens (e.g., nettles, dandelion greens)
- Water
- Salt

Steps
1. Gather and rinse foraged greens.
2. Boil in a pot of water, adding salt to taste until cooked through.

# Egg Drop Broth
A quick and protein-rich meal ready in minutes.

Ingredients
- Boiling broth
- Egg
- Salt

Steps
1. Crack an egg into a pot of simmering broth.
2. Stir gently until the egg is cooked through.
3. Season with a pinch of salt.

# Bread and Ale Soup (Ale Berry)
A hearty breakfast or supper combining bread and ale for a filling meal.

Ingredients
- Stale bread
- Warm ale
- (Optional: Honey or salt)

Steps
1. Crumble stale bread into a bowl.
2. Pour warm ale over bread and let it soak.
3. Add honey or a dash of salt for taste.

# Rendered Fat and Root Vegetables
A high-energy dish cooked simply with root vegetables.

Ingredients
- Turnips or parsnips
- Lard or tallow
- Salt

Steps
1. Slice root vegetables and place them in a pot with rendered fat.
2. Cook over low heat until soft and caramelized.
3. Season with salt.

# Honey Water with Bread
The simplest meal that offers energy and sweetness.

Ingredients
- Bread
- Water
- Honey

Steps
1. Dissolve honey in water to create a sweet liquid.
2. Dunk pieces of bread into the honey water and enjoy.
