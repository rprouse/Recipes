# Warm Couscous Salad with Apricot Vinaigrette

Inspired by the Moroccan way of blending savory and sweet, this couscous salad pairs scallions, fresh herbs, and crunchy almonds with a tangy-sweet apricot vinaigrette. Light, quick, and easy to pack for a picnic or BBQ, it's delicious served warm or at room temperature — perfect alongside grilled Moroccan chicken or almost any chicken, pork, or lamb dish.

### 🛒 Ingredients
- 1½ cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth, best quality such as Swanson
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
- Salt
- 1 10-ounce box couscous (1½ cups)
- 6 tablespoons apricot preserves, best quality such as Bonne Maman
- 4½ tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 tablespoons white wine vinegar
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 2 scallions, white and green parts, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons fresh chopped parsley or mint (optional)
- ⅓ cup sliced almonds

### 🥣 Steps
1. Bring the broth, butter, and ¼ teaspoon salt to a boil in a medium pot. Add the couscous and stir. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and remove from the heat. Let sit for 5 minutes, then fluff the couscous immediately with a fork so it doesn't clump together.
2. In a small bowl, whisk together the apricot preserves, olive oil, vinegar, ¼ teaspoon salt, and a few grinds of black pepper. Add about three-quarters of the vinaigrette to the couscous and stir to combine. Taste and add the remaining vinaigrette little by little if necessary (you may not need it all). Be sure to add all the little chunks of apricot from the vinaigrette, as they tend to settle at the bottom of the bowl.
3. Stir in the scallions, herbs, and sliced almonds. Taste and adjust seasoning (a good bit of salt usually helps) and serve warm or at room temperature.

> [!tip] Tip
> Make-ahead: the salad can be made up to 2 days ahead and stored covered in the refrigerator; add the almonds right before serving so they stay crunchy.
